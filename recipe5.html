Ravioli di Ricotta e Spinaci

Ravioli di Ricotta e Spinaci is a beloved Italian pasta dish, known for its delicate balance of flavors and textures. Originating in the northern regions of Italy, particularly in areas like Emilia-Romagna and Tuscany, this dish has become a classic staple throughout the country and around the world. The name "ravioli" refers to the square or round pasta pockets that are traditionally stuffed with a variety of fillings, and in this particular variety, the filling consists of two key ingredients: creamy ricotta cheese and fresh spinach.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1-2 tbsp water (if needed)
  • 200g (7 oz) fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 200g (7 oz) ricotta cheese
  • Nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg yolk
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 6-8 fresh sage leaves or 1 cup marinara sauce

INSTRUCTIONS

STEP 1: Mix flour and salt in a bowl. Create a well in the center, add eggs, and mix until a dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth. Wrap and let rest for 30 minutes.

STEP 2: Sauté spinach in olive oil until wilted. Let it cool, then chop finely. Mix spinach, ricotta, Parmesan, egg yolk, salt, pepper, and nutmeg in a bowl until combined.

STEP 3: Roll out the dough into thin sheets (about 1mm thick). Use a pasta machine if available.

STEP 4: Place small mounds of filling (about 1 tsp each) spaced evenly on one sheet of pasta. Brush the edges and spaces between mounds with water, then lay another sheet on top. Press around the filling to seal and cut into individual ravioli with a cutter or knife.

STEP 5: Boil a large pot of salted water and cook ravioli for 3–4 minutes or until they float.

STEP 6: Melt butter in a pan, add sage leaves, and cook until fragrant, or heat marinara sauce until warm.

STEP 7: Toss ravioli gently in the butter-sage sauce or spoon marinara over them. Garnish with Parmesan if desired.
