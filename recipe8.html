Sauerbraten

Sauerbraten has deep roots in German culinary tradition, with variations found across many regions of the country. It is particularly popular in southern Germany, especially in regions like Franconia and Swabia, but it’s also common in parts of Hessen and Rhineland. The word "Sauerbraten" literally translates to "sour roast," referring to the marinade's tangy base, which traditionally includes vinegar. The dish was historically prepared with a variety of meats, but beef is the most common today, while pork, lamb, or even horse meat were used in the past.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp mustard seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp cloves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp allspice berries
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2.5 lbs beef roast (e.g., chuck or round)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/4 cup ginger snap cookies, crushed (optional for thickening)

INSTRUCTIONS

STEP 1: In a large bowl, combine all marinade ingredients. Add the beef roast, ensuring it's fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for 3–5 days, turning the meat once a day to marinate evenly.

STEP 2: Remove the beef from the marinade (reserve the marinade for cooking).Pat the beef dry with paper towels. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat and brown the beef on all sides. Remove the beef and set aside.

STEP 3: In the same pot, sauté the chopped onions until softened and slightly browned.

STEP 4: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another minute. Pour in the reserved marinade and beef broth, then return the beef to the pot. Add the raisins and crushed ginger snaps if using.

STEP 5: Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook on low heat for 2.5–3 hours, or until the beef is tender. You can also cook it in the oven at 325°F (163°C) for the same amount of time.

STEP 6: Remove the beef from the pot and slice it against the grain. If the sauce is too thin, simmer it uncovered to reduce or thicken with more crushed ginger snaps.

STEP 7: Serve the Sauerbraten with the sauce poured over the sliced beef, alongside red cabbage, mashed potatoes, or dumplings.
