Paella Valenciana

Paella has its roots in the Albufera wetlands near Valencia, where rice cultivation has been an integral part of the region’s agriculture for centuries. The name "paella" is derived from the Latin word "patella," meaning a shallow pan, which is how the dish is typically prepared and served. Originally, Paella Valenciana was a dish made by farmers and fishermen who would cook it outdoors over an open fire, using locally available ingredients.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 500g (1 lb) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup green beans (e.g., flat green beans or runner beans)
  • 1/2 cup garrofó beans (lima beans can substitute)
  • 2 medium tomatoes, grated
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp saffron threads (soaked in 2 tbsp warm water)
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups short-grain rice (e.g., Bomba or Arborio)
  • Salt to taste
    • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (for flavor)
  • 1/2 cup artichoke hearts or red bell peppers (sliced)

INSTRUCTIONS

STEP 1: Heat olive oil in a large paella pan over medium-high heat. Add chicken and rabbit pieces to the pan. Cook until browned on all sides, then season with salt.

STEP 2: Add green beans, garrofó beans, and optional vegetables like artichokes or bell peppers. Sauté for 5–7 minutes.

STEP 3: Push the meat and vegetables to the sides of the pan. Add grated tomatoes to the center and cook until reduced. Stir in smoked paprika and mix everything together.

STEP 4: Pour in chicken stock and the saffron-infused water. Bring to a boil. Adjust salt to taste and add the rosemary sprig if using.

STEP 5: Sprinkle the rice evenly across the pan without stirring.Reduce heat to medium and let simmer uncovered for about 18–20 minutes, until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is cooked.

STEP 6: Increase heat for the last 1–2 minutes to develop a crispy rice layer at the bottom. Be careful not to burn it.

STEP 7: Remove from heat and let the paella rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with lemon wedges if desired, and serve directly from the pan.
